# Power Bi Jobs Dashboard

![Dashboard Page 1](/images/project_1_page_1.PNG)
## Introduction

This dashboard was created through following **Luke Barousse's Youtube Course for Power Bi**, the dashboard is aimed at **Job seekers, Job Transitioners, and Job Swappers**. The main aim is to solve a common problem: information about the data job market is scattered and hard to grasp. Using a real world dataset of 2024 data science job postings *(including titles, salaries and locations)*, this project provides job seekers a single, easy-to-use interface to explore data related market trends and compensation.


## Skills Learned

- **⚙️ Extract Transform and Loading data with Power Query:**
Cleaned, shaped and prepared data for analysis by handling blanks, working and adjusting different data types and also creating new columns to aid in analysis.
- **🧮 Implicit Measures:**
Formulated measures to draw insights and KPIs like `Median Yearly Salary` and `Job Count`.
- **📊 Core Charts:**
Utilized **Column, Bar, Line and Area Charts** to compare and track job trends over time.
- **🗺️ Geospatial Analysis:**
Leveraged **Map Charts** to visualize the global distribution of jobs.
- **🔢 KPI Indicators and Tables:** Used **Cards** to display key metrics and **Tables** to provide granular, sortable data.
- **🎨 Dashboard Design:**
Designed a visually appealing and intuitive layout, employing both commonly used and uncommon charts to best tell the story behind the data.
- **🖱️ Interactive Reporting:**
    - **Slicers:**
    To dynamically filter the report by Job Title.
    - **Buttons and Bookmarks:**
    To create a seamless navigation experience
    - **Drill Through:**
    To navigate from a high leveel summary to a detailed and contextual view.

## Dashboard Overview

### Page 1: High Level Market View

![Dashboard Page 1](/images/project_1_page_1.PNG)

This section of the dashboard is the control center for the data job market analysis. It showcases key KPIs like total job count, median salaries (Yearly, Hourly) and top job titles to give a quick understanding of the state of the jb market.

### Page 2: Job Title Drill Through

![Dashboard Page 2](/images/project_1_page_2.PNG)

This page is where you get to view specific metrics and KPIs for a single job title in the data jobs market. You cna drill through to view the median salaries (Hourly or Yearly) , the work from home percentage , any mention of degrees in the job posting or the percentage of job psotings  that provide health insurance. Lastly ther are also charts that show which platform the job is msot posted, which locationa and the job types.
